My favorite SciFi Books

Let’s start with books. I must admit that until recently, I have never read a SciFi book before. This will change now after I read Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari.

Book Description

2014 - Sapiens - A Brief History of Humankind

Harari's way of summarizing the story of humankind in a precise, humorist and fact-based way was fascinating and it gave me so many new perspectives on my personal life that I was absolutely overwhelmed by him describing the past. But his way of picturing the future in his last chapter was mind-blowing. I already referred to the chapter in the first step of the application process. Harari states that we have reached a time when human beings have actually evolved into a “god- like status” because we start to replace the laws of natural evolution/selection with the laws of natural design. In the future, we will see increased biological engineering (changing the DNA), cyborg engineering (combination of organic and non-organic parts) and the engineering of inorganic life (machines with consciousness). This chapter is one of the reasons why I want to dive deeper into AI and I am excited about Harari’s second book “Homo Deus” that covers the future only! I got it as a birthday present and I will directly start reading it after having finished “Originals” by Adam Grant.

My favorite SciFi Movies

On the movie side, I have some more to show. Here are some of my favorite SciFi movies and how they influenced me:

Movie Description

2014 - Interstellar

Don’t take things for granted, change the status quo. (In this case the time that was considered as irreversible)

2010 - Inception

Amazing movie because of its complexity and open end (not knowing whether Cobb is still in level 1 dream or back in reality). It made me think a lot about the human brain. On the one hand, how powerful it became over the centuries and on the other hand, how much more unrevealed potential there still must be.

2015 - The Marsian

A really exciting and funny movie. The messages transported are more or less the maxim on which I act: Never give up, no matter how desperate the situation might be. Make the most of it! Be creative, you would be surprised what you can achieve with little input! And: You only have one life, so do something that really matters!

2009 - Avatar

(+ Star Wars) No deeper meaning for me but just because both movies started a new era of technology in film.

My favorite SciFi TV Series

TV Series Description

2011 - Black Mirror

Last but not least: Black Mirror. I have only been watching the first couple of episodes but I must say that this TV series really gets into your head. Although I am a digital native and in favor of innovation, I think it’s good to also address the worst possible side effects of technological innovation.
